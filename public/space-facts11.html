☀️ The Solar System

Our cosmic neighborhood of 8 planets, countless moons, and vast asteroid belts

☀️ The Sun - Our Star

The Sun is a massive ball of hot plasma that accounts for 99.86% of all mass in our solar system. It has been burning for 4.6 billion years and will continue for another 5 billion years.

Sun Facts:

  • Surface Temperature: 10,000°F (5,500°C)
  • Core Temperature: 27 million°F
  • Diameter: 864,000 miles
  • 1.3 million Earths could fit inside
  • Converts 600 million tons of hydrogen/second

🪨 Asteroids, Meteors & Meteorites

Rocky debris scattered throughout the solar system

🪐 The Asteroid Belt

Located between Mars and Jupiter, the asteroid belt contains millions of rocky objects left over from the formation of the solar system.

Asteroid Belt Facts:

  • Location: Between Mars and Jupiter orbits
  • Contains: Millions of asteroids
  • Largest object: Ceres (dwarf planet, 945 km diameter)
  • Total mass: Less than our Moon
  • Spacing: Asteroids are far apart (avg 600,000 km apart)
  • Age: 4.6 billion years
  • Composition: Rock and metal
🪨 Ceres
Dwarf planet, largest object in asteroid belt, 25% of belt's mass
💫 Vesta
Second largest, about 530 km diameter, brightest asteroid
🔴 Pallas
Third largest, irregular shape, 510 km diameter
⭐ Juno
About 250 km diameter, unusual composition

🌠 Meteors & Meteorites

Understanding the Difference:

  • Meteoroid: Small rocky object in space
  • Meteor: Meteoroid burning up in atmosphere (shooting star)
  • Meteorite: Meteor that reaches Earth's surface
🌟 Major Meteor Showers ▼

Quadrantids: January (40 meteors/hour)

Perseids: August (100+ meteors/hour) - Most popular

Geminids: December (150 meteors/hour)

Leonids: November (20 meteors/hour)

Lyrids: April (20 meteors/hour)

Meteorite Types:

  • Chondrites: Most common (86%), contain chondrules
  • Achondrites: Iron and stone composition
  • Iron Meteorites: 90% iron, 10% nickel
  • Stony-Iron: Mix of iron and stone

⚫ Black Holes & Extreme Objects

The most extreme objects in the universe

🌌 What Are Black Holes?

Black holes are regions in space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. They form when massive stars collapse at the end of their lives.

Black Hole Facts:

  • Event Horizon: Point of no return around black hole
  • Singularity: Infinitely dense point at black hole center
  • Time Dilation: Time moves slower near black holes
  • Hawking Radiation: Black holes slowly evaporate over time
  • Supermassive: Found at centers of most galaxies
  • Detection: Observed through X-rays and gravitational waves
🔭 Notable Black Holes ▼

Sagittarius A*
Supermassive black hole at center of Milky Way, 4.1 million solar masses

M87
First image captured (2019), 6.5 billion solar masses, in Virgo galaxy

Cygnus X-1
Stellar black hole, 15 solar masses, feeds on companion star
